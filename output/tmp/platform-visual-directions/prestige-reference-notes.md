# Direction A

- What creates perceived quality: Cool off-white space, direct customer-facing copy, a restrained navy/teal palette, and generous spacing.
- What creates depth: Three overlapping paper planes, soft shadows, a mint field, and one curved routing line.
- How visual hierarchy is established: A very large sans-serif headline, one teal phrase, then a dark primary action and a smaller explanatory link.
- How motion is used: Only small hover movement and the mobile navigation state; the hero object does not meaningfully assemble or change.
- How imagery or product visuals are used: The product story is compressed into a CSS-built paper stack and small diagrammatic interface fragments.
- What must not be copied literally: The split hero, paper-stack artwork, single-font expression, small diagram scale, flat section bands, or repeated card sections.
